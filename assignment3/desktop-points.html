Points

Points work the exact same way Cash Back works, but the only difference is that you get a specific number of points instead of cash.

Who can get them?

Why do people get them?

What are their purpose?

Most mid-tier credit cards, offer points instead of CB. Points can be used just like CB, but they could also, be used for many other purposes such as buying flights or anything that could be purchase with points.

Points are sometimes worth more than CB in some aspect. They hold so much more value when pairing them up for like flights or purchases. While using your card regularly, you can earn so many points that you can technically buy stuff for free. For instance, if you earn 1 point for $1 use, and you use $100 you will get 100 points. Sometimes you can earn even more than 1 point per dollar.

Points are sometimes worth more than CB depending on the amount. For instance, if you have 70,000 points, and you want to redeem them for cash. Most issuers value their points to about $.01 per point, which is about $700 in the end, but there are times when points can be valued up to about $.02 to $.05 per point which if going with the 5 cents, is about $3,500.
